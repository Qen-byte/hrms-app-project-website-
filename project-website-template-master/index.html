It addresses challenges in employee management, payroll processing, performance management, and interdepartmental communication by providing a user-friendly platform built with Flutter and integrated with Firebase for secure data management

Overview

Organizations often face difficulties in managing employee data, processing payroll efficiently, maintaining clear communication across departments, and managing employee performance. Traditional methods can be time-consuming and prone to errors, impacting overall productivity and employee satisfaction. Additionally, the challenge of performance management includes the need for employees to track their activities and keep track of their progress, which is crucial for personal development and organizational growth. Another significant challenge is attendance tracking, ensuring that employees are present and their attendance is accurately recorded.

User Types

Administrator

Administrators have full access to all features of the HRMS app. Their responsibilities include managing user accounts, configuring system settings, overseeing attendance, and generating reports. Administrators can access all modules and features within the application.

Employee

Employees can manage their personal information, track attendance, request time off, and view performance metrics. They have access to features relevant to their roles and receive notifications and updates pertaining to their work.

Manager

Managers can oversee the performance and attendance of their team members, approve leave requests, and provide feedback. They have access to tools and reports specific to their team and can manage team-related tasks.

HR Specialist

HR Specialists handle employee records, recruitment, onboarding, and policy enforcement. They manage HR-related processes and generate reports and analytics related to human resources.

Authentication

The HRMS app uses a robust authentication system to ensure secure access to user accounts and sensitive data. Below is an overview of the authentication methods and security practices:

Authentication Methods

Username and Password

Registration: Users can create an account by providing a username, password, and email address. Passwords must meet the following requirements: minimum 8 characters, including at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, and one number.

Login: To access their accounts, users enter their username and password on the login page.

Password Reset: If a user forgets their password, they can request a password reset link sent to their registered email. Follow the instructions in the email to set a new password.

Email Link Authentication (Magic Link)

Process: Users can log in by requesting a magic link to be sent to their email address. Clicking the link logs them in directly without needing a password.

Advantages: This method enhances security by reducing password-related vulnerabilities and simplifies the login process for users.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Setup: Users can enable 2FA by linking their account with an authentication app or by receiving SMS codes. Follow the in-app instructions to set up 2FA.

Login with 2FA: Users will be prompted to enter a code from their authentication app or received via SMS as an additional step during login.

Account Security

Password Requirements: Passwords must be at least 8 characters long, with a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, and numbers.

Security Practices: Users should avoid using the same password across multiple sites and keep their passwords confidential.

User Session Management

Session Expiration: User sessions automatically expire after 30 minutes of inactivity, requiring re-authentication.

Logout Process: Users can log out by clicking the "Logout" button in the user menu. Ensure to log out from all devices if accessing the app from multiple locations.

Access Control

Role-Based Access: Authentication determines user access based on their role. For example, Administrators have access to all features, while Employees have limited access based on their role.

Permission Management: Users can request access to additional features or changes in permissions by contacting their HR or system administrator.

Error Handling

Common Errors: Common issues include incorrect passwords or locked accounts. Follow the on-screen instructions or refer to the password reset process to resolve these errors.

Contact Support: For any issues with authentication, contact support at [support@example.com] or use the in-app help feature.

Attendance Tracking

The attendance tracking feature in the HRMS app helps monitor and manage employee attendance efficiently. Here’s an overview of the available options and processes:

Attendance Methods

Clock In/Out

Process: Employees can clock in and out using the mobile app or web portal. Each entry is recorded with a timestamp and location (if applicable).

Features: Users can view their clock-in/out history and request corrections if needed. Supervisors can also review attendance records for their team.

QR Code Scanning

Process: Employees scan a QR code at their work location to record their attendance. The system logs the time and location of the scan.

Setup: QR codes are generated and managed through the admin interface, ensuring they are up-to-date and accurate.

Manual Entry

Process: Employees or administrators can manually enter attendance data or make adjustments through the HRMS interface.

Approval: Manual entries and adjustments may require approval from a supervisor or HR specialist before they are finalized.

Absence Management

Leave Requests

Types of Leave: Employees can request various types of leave, including vacation, sick leave, and personal leave.

Process: To request leave, employees submit a request through the HRMS app. The request is reviewed and approved by a manager or HR specialist.

Tracking: Leave requests are tracked and displayed in attendance reports, providing visibility into employee absences.

Attendance Reports

Accessing Reports: Attendance reports can be accessed via the reporting module. Reports include daily attendance records and monthly summaries.

Generating Reports: Custom reports can be generated to meet specific needs, such as analyzing attendance trends or tracking specific employee data.

Alerts and Notifications

Attendance Alerts

Types of Alerts: Alerts may include missed clock-ins, late arrivals, or unauthorized absences.

Notification Settings: Users can configure their notification preferences to receive alerts via email or push notifications.

Integration with Other Systems

Payroll Integration

Purpose: Attendance data integrates with payroll systems to ensure accurate compensation based on recorded hours.

Data Transfer: Attendance data is securely transferred to payroll systems, ensuring consistency and accuracy in employee pay.

HR Management Integration

Purpose: Attendance data is used for performance tracking and other HR functions, providing a comprehensive view of employee activity.

Security and Compliance

Data Security

Protection Measures: Attendance data is protected using encryption and secure access controls to prevent unauthorized access.

Compliance: The system complies with labor laws and regulations, ensuring that attendance tracking practices meet legal requirements.

Types of Graphs

Usage: Bar graphs are used to display categorical data, such as the number of employees in different departments or the number of leave requests per month.

Examples: Examples include visualizing employee distribution by department or comparing leave request volumes across different periods.

Line Graphs

Usage: Line graphs track changes over time, such as attendance trends or performance metrics, showing how these variables evolve.

Examples: Examples include monthly attendance rates or quarterly performance review scores.

Pie Charts

Usage: Pie charts represent data as a percentage of a whole, such as the distribution of employees across different departments or the proportion of different types of leave.

Examples: Examples include showing the proportion of employees in various departments or the breakdown of leave types taken.

Histograms

Usage: Histograms show the distribution of numerical data, such as employee age ranges or tenure within the company.

Examples: Examples include visualizing the age distribution of employees or the length of service in the company.

Scatter Plots

Usage: Scatter plots illustrate the relationship between two variables, such as performance ratings versus productivity metrics.

Examples: Examples include analyzing the correlation between performance scores and productivity levels.

Integration: Graphs are integrated with data sources within the HRMS app, such as databases or real-time data feeds, ensuring accurate and up-to-date information.

Graph Features
